## 💡 Proposal
As Creative Leader, I propose the implementation of **Graph-Centrality Weighted Context Retrieval** within the `get_context_for_query` function.

### The Problem
Currently, the system performs vector search (taking **621.7ms**) and BM25 search to retrieve context. However, these methods treat all nodes as isolated text chunks. In a "Knowledge Graph" or "Autobrain" context, a node’s structural importance (how often it is linked) is often as important as its semantic similarity to a query. 

### The Improvement: Hybrid Importance Scoring
Integrate the output of `get_wikilink_frequencies` (from `proc_24_main`) into the ranking logic of `get_context_for_query` (from `proc_12_chat`).

**Technical Implementation:**
1.  **Extract Centrality:** Use the existing `get_wikilink_frequencies` logic to generate a normalized "importance score" (0.0 to 1.0) for every node in the graph based on its in-link count.
2.  **Modify the Ranker:** Instead of ranking context purely by Vector Similarity, use a weighted scoring formula:
    `Final_Score = (α * Vector_Similarity) + (β * BM25_Score) + (γ * Wikilink_Frequency_Score)`
3.  **Contextual Re-ranking:** When `build_system_prompt` is called, it will receive nodes that are not only semantically relevant but also represent "hubs" of information in the user's brain.

### Why this is useful:
*   **Surfaces "Hub" Concepts:** If a user asks about a broad topic, the system will prioritize "Map of Content" nodes or high-level summaries that have many links, rather than obscure, low-link notes that happen to share a few keywords.
*   **Mitigates FTS Issues:** Since your Full Text Search (FTS) indexes are currently missing/degraded (as per the warning), leveraging the **graph structure** as a ranking signal compensates for the temporary weakness in keyword-based retrieval.
*   **Efficiency:** It leverages data already being processed in `proc_24_main`, turning static frequency counts into dynamic retrieval filters.

This shift transforms the system from a standard RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) into a **Graph-RAG**, significantly improving the "intelligence" of the `build_system_prompt` output.
