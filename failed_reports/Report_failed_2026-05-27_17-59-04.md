## 💡 Proposal
To optimize the intelligence and performance of the retrieval system, I propose implementing **Graph-Weighted Semantic Re-ranking**.

### The Problem
Currently, context retrieval (`proc_16_chat`) relies on a hybrid of BM25 (14.5ms) and Vector search (623.2ms). While semantic search is powerful, it often retrieves "flat" text snippets that lack structural importance. Furthermore, the 623ms vector latency is the primary bottleneck in the system's "wall time."

### The Solution: Graph-Weighted Re-ranking
We should modify `get_context_for_query` to integrate the output of `get_wikilink_frequencies` (from `proc_22_main`) as a re-ranking signal. 

**Technical Implementation:**
1.  **Centrality Boosting:** When `get_context_for_query` retrieves a set of candidate nodes, cross-reference their IDs with the frequency map generated by `get_wikilink_frequencies`.
2.  **Scoring Formula:** Apply a weighted score to each candidate: 
    `Final_Score = (Semantic_Similarity * w1) + (Normalized_Wikilink_Frequency * w2)`.
3.  **Context Pruning:** Use this score to aggressively prune low-signal "ghost links" (utilizing `remove_isolated_wikilinks`) before the context is passed to `build_system_prompt`.

### Why this is the priority:
*   **Structural Intelligence:** It elevates "Central Nodes" (topics the user mentions/links to often) over peripheral mentions, ensuring the AI's response is grounded in the most established parts of the user's knowledge graph.
*   **Validation Alignment:** It aligns retrieval with your existing quality rules in `validator.py` (like `_rule_has_wikilinks`), ensuring the chat doesn't just get "relevant" text, but "high-quality, interconnected" text.
*   **Efficiency:** By using graph frequency (a lightweight lookup) to filter the top results, we can potentially reduce the number of tokens sent to the LLM, offsetting the latency of the vector search.

This turns your retrieval from a simple search engine into a **Graph-Aware RAG** (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) system.
