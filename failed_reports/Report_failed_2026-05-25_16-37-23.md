## 💡 Proposal
As Creative Leader, I propose the implementation of **Link-Density Weighted Retrieval (LDWR)** within the `graph_manager.py` module.

### The Problem
Currently, the system retrieves context via `get_context_for_query` (the highest priority process, `proc_16_chat`) and separately tracks `get_wikilink_frequencies` (the second highest priority, `proc_22_main`). These two high-value streams are currently siloed. Retrieval likely relies on standard semantic similarity, which can sometimes surface "orphan" notes or low-value fragments that happen to match keywords but lack depth or connectivity.

### The Solution: Link-Density Weighted Retrieval (LDWR)
We should modify `autobrain_core/graph_manager.py:get_context_for_query` to apply a **centrality multiplier** to the search results. By injecting the data already calculated by `get_wikilink_frequencies`, we can prioritize "Knowledge Hubs" in the AI's context window.

**Technical Implementation:**
1.  **Score Injection:** During the ranking phase of `get_context_for_query`, fetch the frequency count of wikilinks for the candidate nodes.
2.  **The Weighted Rank:** Apply a non-linear boost to the node's relevance score:
    `Final_Score = Semantic_Similarity * (1 + log(Wikilink_Frequency + 1))`
3.  **Validation Feedback Loop:** Utilize the existing `validator.py` rules (specifically `_rule_has_wikilinks`) as a pre-filter. If a node is retrieved but has zero wikilinks and fails the "ghost link" check, it is deprioritized in favor of nodes that anchor the knowledge graph.

### Why this is the priority:
*   **Improves RAG Quality:** It ensures the AI receives the most "vetted" and "connected" information first, rather than obscure, isolated snippets.
*   **Leverages Existing Logic:** It synthesizes two existing high-priority processes (`proc_16` and `proc_22`) into a single competitive advantage.
*   **Mitigates Degraded Search:** Since your FTS (Full Text Search) indexes are currently missing/degraded (as per the warning), weighting by graph connectivity provides a structural fallback to ensure the most important nodes are still found via their relationships.

This improvement transforms the system from a simple document retriever into a true **"Neural Brain"** that understands which concepts are its own pillars.
