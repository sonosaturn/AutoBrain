As the Creative Leader, I propose the implementation of a **Graph-Weighted Semantic Reranker** to bridge the gap between raw vector retrieval and the structural intelligence of your knowledge graph.

### The Proposal: Link-Density Contextual Scoring
Currently, `proc_16_chat` retrieves context for queries using vector search, which accounts for **85% of your total wall time (904ms)**. However, vector search alone often retrieves "semantically similar" but structurally "isolated" nodes that may not be useful for the AI’s response.

We should implement a reranking layer between `get_context_for_query` and `build_system_prompt` that adjusts the relevance score of retrieved nodes based on their **Wikilink Frequency** (leveraging your existing `get_wikilink_frequencies` logic).

#### Technical Implementation:
1.  **Extract Graph Weight:** For every node retrieved by the vector search, query its "in-degree" or frequency within the graph using the logic found in `autobrain_core/materialize_nodes.py`.
2.  **Calculate a Hybrid Score:** Apply a weighting formula:
    $$Score_{final} = (w_1 \cdot VectorSimilarity) + (w_2 \cdot \log(WikilinkFrequency + 1))$$
3.  **Active Pruning:** Automatically discard nodes that trigger the `remove_isolated_wikilinks` threshold, ensuring that "ghost links" or low-value nodes never enter the `build_system_prompt` phase.

#### Why this is useful:
*   **Higher Signal-to-Noise:** The system prompt will prioritize "central" concepts in the user's brain over peripheral, low-context notes that happen to share keyword overlap.
*   **Mitigates Degraded FTS:** Since your Full-Text Search (FTS) indexes are currently missing/degraded (per the warning), this hybrid approach uses the graph's structure as a secondary validation for relevance, making the system more resilient to keyword search failures.
*   **Latency Optimization:** By reranking and pruning *before* the context is sent to the LLM, you can reduce the token count in `build_system_prompt`, potentially lowering inference costs and response time.
